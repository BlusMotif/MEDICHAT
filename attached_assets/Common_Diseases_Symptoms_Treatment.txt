

# 50 COMMON DISEASES IN AFRICA WITH SYMPTOMS, DIAGNOSIS, AND TREATMENT

## 1. MALARIA
- **Symptoms**: High fever, chills, sweating, headache, nausea, muscle pain, fatigue.
- **Diagnosis**: Blood smear test, Rapid Diagnostic Test (RDT), Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR).
- **Treatment**: Antimalarial drugs (Artemisinin-based Combination Therapy - ACT), supportive care for fever and dehydration.

## 2. TYPHOID FEVER
- **Symptoms**: Prolonged high fever, abdominal pain, loss of appetite, diarrhea or constipation, fatigue, rash (rose spots).
- **Diagnosis**: Widal test, blood culture, stool and urine analysis.
- **Treatment**: Antibiotics (Ciprofloxacin, Azithromycin), hydration therapy.

## 3. CHOLERA
- **Symptoms**: Severe watery diarrhea, dehydration, muscle cramps, vomiting.
- **Diagnosis**: Stool culture, Rapid Cholera Dipstick Test.
- **Treatment**: Oral Rehydration Therapy (ORT), intravenous fluids, antibiotics (Doxycycline, Azithromycin).

## 4. TUBERCULOSIS (TB)
- **Symptoms**: Persistent cough (lasting more than 3 weeks), weight loss, night sweats, fever, fatigue, chest pain.
- **Diagnosis**: Tuberculin skin test (Mantoux), sputum smear microscopy, chest X-ray, GeneXpert test.
- **Treatment**: 6-month course of antibiotics (Rifampin, Isoniazid, Ethambutol, Pyrazinamide).

## 5. HEPATITIS B
- **Symptoms**: Jaundice (yellowing of skin and eyes), dark urine, fatigue, nausea, abdominal pain.
- **Diagnosis**: Blood test for Hepatitis B surface antigen (HBsAg), liver function tests.
- **Treatment**: Antiviral medications (Tenofovir, Entecavir), liver monitoring, supportive therapy.

## 6. YELLOW FEVER
- **Symptoms**: Fever, chills, muscle pain, nausea, vomiting, jaundice, bleeding from gums or nose.
- **Diagnosis**: Blood test for yellow fever virus (PCR or ELISA test).
- **Treatment**: Supportive care (fluids, pain relievers, rest). Preventable by vaccination.

## 7. LASSA FEVER
- **Symptoms**: Fever, sore throat, chest pain, bleeding (nose, gums), abdominal pain, shock in severe cases.
- **Diagnosis**: Lassa virus blood test (PCR), ELISA test.
- **Treatment**: Ribavirin antiviral therapy, supportive care.

## 8. MENINGITIS
- **Symptoms**: Stiff neck, severe headache, fever, nausea, sensitivity to light, confusion.
- **Diagnosis**: Lumbar puncture (spinal tap), blood tests, CT scan.
- **Treatment**: Antibiotics for bacterial meningitis, antiviral treatment for viral meningitis, corticosteroids to reduce inflammation.

## 9. DIARRHEAL DISEASES (E.G. ROTAVIRUS, E. COLI INFECTION)
- **Symptoms**: Frequent loose stools, dehydration, stomach cramps, fever, nausea.
- **Diagnosis**: Stool analysis, rapid antigen test.
- **Treatment**: Oral Rehydration Salts (ORS), zinc supplements, antibiotics if bacterial cause is confirmed.

## 10. MEASLES
- **Symptoms**: High fever, runny nose, cough, red eyes, rash that spreads from the face downwards.
- **Diagnosis**: Clinical observation, blood test for measles IgM antibodies.
- **Treatment**: Supportive care (fluids, fever reducers, vitamin A supplements). Preventable by vaccination.

## 11. HIV/AIDS
- **Symptoms**: Swollen lymph nodes, fever, chronic diarrhea, weight loss, night sweats, skin rashes.
- **Diagnosis**: HIV rapid test, ELISA test, CD4 count.
- **Treatment**: Antiretroviral therapy (ART) to manage the virus and prevent complications.



## 13. SCHISTOSOMIASIS (BILHARZIA)
- **Symptoms**: Bloody urine, abdominal pain, enlarged liver and spleen, itchy skin rash.
- **Diagnosis**: Urine or stool microscopy, blood test.
- **Treatment**: Praziquantel (antiparasitic medication).

## 14. ONCHOCERCIASIS (RIVER BLINDNESS)
- **Symptoms**: Skin rash, intense itching, nodules under the skin, progressive blindness.
- **Diagnosis**: Skin snip test, blood test.
- **Treatment**: Ivermectin (antiparasitic drug), doxycycline for secondary infections.

## 15. LEPROSY
- **Symptoms**: Skin lesions, loss of sensation in affected areas, nerve damage, muscle weakness.
- **Diagnosis**: Skin biopsy, clinical examination.
- **Treatment**: Multi-drug therapy (MDT) with Rifampin, Dapsone, and Clofazimine.

## 16. RABIES
- **Symptoms**: Fever, hydrophobia (fear of water), muscle spasms, confusion, paralysis.
- **Diagnosis**: Skin biopsy, saliva test, cerebrospinal fluid analysis.
- **Treatment**: No cure after symptom onset, but rabies vaccine and immunoglobulin prevent onset if given immediately after exposure.

## 17. DENGUE FEVER
- **Symptoms**: High fever, severe joint and muscle pain, rash, bleeding gums.
- **Diagnosis**: Blood test for dengue virus.
- **Treatment**: Supportive care (fluids, pain relievers like acetaminophen). Preventable by mosquito control.

## 18. SICKLE CELL DISEASE
- **Symptoms**: Severe pain episodes, anemia, swelling in hands and feet, frequent infections, vision problems.
- **Diagnosis**: Blood test, hemoglobin electrophoresis.
- **Treatment**: Pain management, blood transfusions, hydroxyurea therapy.

## 19. PNEUMONIA
- **Symptoms**: Cough with phlegm, chest pain, fever, difficulty breathing, fatigue.
- **Diagnosis**: Chest X-ray, sputum test, blood test.
- **Treatment**: Antibiotics for bacterial pneumonia, antivirals for viral pneumonia, oxygen therapy if needed.

## 20. BRUCELLOSIS
- **Symptoms**: Fever, joint pain, fatigue, muscle aches, weight loss.
- **Diagnosis**: Blood test, culture test.
- **Treatment**: Antibiotics (Doxycycline and Rifampin for six weeks).

## 21. FILARIASIS
- **Symptoms**: Swelling of limbs (elephantiasis), skin thickening, fever, rashes.
- **Diagnosis**: Blood smear test.
- **Treatment**: Antiparasitic drugs (Diethylcarbamazine, Ivermectin).

## 22. LEISHMANIASIS
- **Symptoms**: Skin sores, weight loss, fever, swollen liver and spleen.
- **Diagnosis**: Tissue biopsy, blood test.
- **Treatment**: Antimonial drugs, Amphotericin B.

## 23. INFLUENZA (FLU)
- **Symptoms**: Fever, chills, body aches, sore throat, cough, congestion, fatigue.
- **Diagnosis**: Rapid influenza diagnostic test (RIDT), PCR test.
- **Treatment**: Antiviral medications (Oseltamivir, Zanamivir), rest, hydration.

## 24. HYPERTENSION (HIGH BLOOD PRESSURE)
- **Symptoms**: Often asymptomatic, but can cause headaches, dizziness, blurred vision, chest pain.
- **Diagnosis**: Blood pressure measurement.
- **Treatment**: Lifestyle changes (diet, exercise), antihypertensive medications.

## 25. DIABETES MELLITUS
- **Symptoms**: Frequent urination, increased thirst, unexplained weight loss, fatigue, blurred vision.
- **Diagnosis**: Blood glucose test, HbA1c test.
- **Treatment**: Insulin therapy (for Type 1), oral hypoglycemic drugs (for Type 2), dietary management.

## 26. GASTROENTERITIS
- **Symptoms**: Diarrhea, vomiting, stomach cramps, fever, dehydration.
- **Diagnosis**: Stool sample test.
- **Treatment**: Fluid replacement, antibiotics if bacterial infection is confirmed.

## 27. ASTHMA
- **Symptoms**: Shortness of breath, wheezing, chest tightness, chronic cough.
- **Diagnosis**: Pulmonary function test (Spirometry), peak flow measurement.
- **Treatment**: Inhalers (Bronchodilators like Salbutamol), corticosteroids.

## 28. PEPTIC ULCER DISEASE
- **Symptoms**: Burning stomach pain, bloating, nausea, weight loss.
- **Diagnosis**: Endoscopy, H. pylori test.
- **Treatment**: Proton pump inhibitors (Omeprazole), antibiotics for H. pylori.

## 29. HEPATITIS C
- **Symptoms**: Jaundice, dark urine, fatigue, nausea, abdominal pain.
- **Diagnosis**: Blood test for Hepatitis C antibodies.
- **Treatment**: Antiviral medications (Sofosbuvir, Ledipasvir).

## 30. TRACHOMA
- **Symptoms**: Eye irritation, watery discharge, redness, blindness in severe cases.
- **Diagnosis**: Clinical eye examination, PCR test.
- **Treatment**: Antibiotics (Azithromycin), improved hygiene practices.

## 31. SYPHILIS
- **Symptoms**: Painless sores, rashes, swollen lymph nodes, fever.
- **Diagnosis**: Blood test (VDRL, RPR test).
- **Treatment**: Penicillin injections.

## 32. GONORRHEA
- **Symptoms**: Painful urination, discharge, pelvic pain, swollen testicles in men.
- **Diagnosis**: Urine test, swab test.
- **Treatment**: Antibiotics (Ceftriaxone, Azithromycin).

## 33. HERPES SIMPLEX VIRUS (HSV)
- **Symptoms**: Painful blisters around the mouth or genitals, fever, swollen lymph nodes.
- **Diagnosis**: Blood test, PCR test.
- **Treatment**: Antiviral drugs (Acyclovir, Valacyclovir).

## 34. LEPTOSPIROSIS
- **Symptoms**: Fever, muscle aches, chills, jaundice, kidney failure in severe cases.
- **Diagnosis**: Blood or urine culture.
- **Treatment**: Antibiotics (Doxycycline, Penicillin).

## 35. DERMATOPHYTOSIS (RINGWORM)
- **Symptoms**: Circular red rashes, itching, hair loss (in scalp infections).
- **Diagnosis**: Skin scraping test.
- **Treatment**: Antifungal creams, oral antifungal medications.

## 36. SCABIES
- **Symptoms**: Intense itching, red burrow tracks on skin, skin inflammation.
- **Diagnosis**: Skin scraping test.
- **Treatment**: Topical Permethrin cream, Ivermectin for severe cases.

## 37. TETANUS
- **Symptoms**: Muscle stiffness, spasms, jaw lock, difficulty swallowing.
- **Diagnosis**: Clinical examination.
- **Treatment**: Tetanus vaccine, antibiotics, muscle relaxants.

## 38. PERTUSSIS (WHOOPING COUGH)
- **Symptoms**: Severe coughing fits, vomiting after coughing, runny nose.
- **Diagnosis**: Nasopharyngeal swab test.
- **Treatment**: Antibiotics (Azithromycin, Erythromycin).

## 39. DYSENTERY (AMOEBIC & BACTERIAL)
- **Symptoms**: Bloody diarrhea, fever, stomach cramps, dehydration.
- **Diagnosis**: Stool culture, microscopy.
- **Treatment**: Rehydration therapy, antibiotics for bacterial dysentery.

## 40. TONSILLITIS
- **Symptoms**: Sore throat, swollen tonsils, fever, difficulty swallowing.
- **Diagnosis**: Throat swab test.
- **Treatment**: Pain relievers, antibiotics if bacterial.

## 41. STROKE
- **Symptoms**: Sudden numbness, slurred speech, difficulty walking, severe headache.
- **Diagnosis**: CT scan, MRI.
- **Treatment**: Clot-busting drugs (for ischemic stroke), blood pressure control.

## 42. LEAD POISONING
- **Symptoms**: Abdominal pain, developmental delays in children, irritability, anemia.
- **Diagnosis**: Blood lead level test.
- **Treatment**: Chelation therapy.

## 43. CHAGAS DISEASE
- **Symptoms**: Fever, swelling at the bite site, heart failure in chronic cases.
- **Diagnosis**: Blood test, PCR test.
- **Treatment**: Antiparasitic drugs (Benznidazole, Nifurtimox).

## 44. OSTEOMYELITIS
- **Symptoms**: Bone pain, fever, redness, swelling over affected bone.
- **Diagnosis**: MRI, bone biopsy.
- **Treatment**: IV antibiotics, surgery if severe.

## 45. APPENDICITIS
- **Symptoms**: Sudden right lower abdominal pain, nausea, fever, vomiting.
- **Diagnosis**: Abdominal ultrasound, CT scan.
- **Treatment**: Surgical removal (Appendectomy).

## 46. POLIO
- **Symptoms**: Muscle weakness, paralysis, fever, headache.
- **Diagnosis**: Stool or throat culture.
- **Treatment**: No cure, supportive therapy, preventable with polio vaccine.

## 47. LYMPHATIC FILARIASIS
- **Symptoms**: Swollen limbs (Elephantiasis), skin thickening, fever.
- **Diagnosis**: Blood smear test.
- **Treatment**: Antiparasitic drugs (Ivermectin, Albendazole).

## 48. SLEEPING SICKNESS (TRYPANOSOMIASIS)
- **Symptoms**: Confusion, fever, sleep disturbances, swollen lymph nodes.
- **Diagnosis**: Blood test, lumbar puncture.
- **Treatment**: Antitrypanosomal drugs (Suramin, Melarsoprol).

## 49. HANTAVIRUS
- **Symptoms**: Fever, muscle aches, shortness of breath, kidney failure in severe cases.
- **Diagnosis**: Blood test.
- **Treatment**: Supportive care.

## 50. BURULI ULCER
- **Symptoms**: Painless ulcer, swelling, tissue destruction.
- **Diagnosis**: PCR test, biopsy.
- **Treatment**: Antibiotics (Rifampin, Streptomycin).
